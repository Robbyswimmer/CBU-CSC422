### 2. Technical Report (10.5 points)

Submit a **3-4 page technical research paper** following academic conference standards:

#### Section 1: Abstract & Introduction (0.5 page)
- **Abstract**: Concise summary of problem, approach, results, and contributions
- **Problem Statement**: Clear research question or application challenge  
- **Motivation**: Why this problem matters to the field or society
- **Contributions**: Specific novel contributions of your work
- **Organization**: Brief outline of paper structure

#### Section 2: Related Work & Background (0.75 page)
- **Literature Review**: Comprehensive review of 5-8 relevant academic papers
- **Technical Background**: Essential concepts and prior approaches
- **Gap Analysis**: Specific limitations of existing work your project addresses
- **Positioning**: How your work builds upon and advances the field
- **Baseline Comparisons**: Existing methods you compare against

#### Section 3: Methodology (1.5 pages)
- **Approach Overview**: High-level description of your solution approach
- **Technical Details**: Detailed algorithm descriptions, architectures, or system design
- **Implementation**: Key implementation decisions and framework choices
- **Experimental Design**: Rigorous experimental methodology and controls
- **Data**: Dataset description, preprocessing, and ethical considerations
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Justification for chosen evaluation approaches

#### Section 4: Experiments & Results (1 page)
- **Experimental Setup**: Hardware, software, and parameter configurations
- **Quantitative Results**: Comprehensive performance analysis with statistical significance
- **Ablation Studies**: Analysis of component contributions (where applicable)
- **Comparative Analysis**: Detailed comparison with baselines and state-of-the-art
- **Error Analysis**: Investigation of failure cases and limitations
- **Visualizations**: Research-quality figures that illuminate key findings

#### Section 5: Discussion & Future Work (0.75 page)
- **Key Insights**: Main findings and their implications for the field
- **Limitations**: Honest assessment of approach limitations and scope
- **Broader Impact**: Societal implications, ethical considerations, potential risks
- **Future Directions**: Specific next steps for research continuation  
- **Lessons Learned**: Reflection on research process and methodology

#### Academic Writing Standards
- **Format**: Conference paper format (IEEE, ACM, or similar)
- **Length**: 3-4 pages excluding references and appendices
- **Citations**: Minimum 8-12 academic references in proper format
- **Figures**: Publication-quality figures with detailed captions
- **Style**: Formal academic writing appropriate for peer review
